# Hero Establishing

## Purpose
Set the product in frame with enough context to establish category, scale, and tone without over-explaining the scene.

## When to use
Use at the opening of a product film or after a hard scene change when the viewer needs orientation before detail work.

## Motion profile
Slow push-in, subtle lateral drift, or a controlled locked-off hold with minimal camera movement. Avoid fast pans, aggressive zooms, or decorative motion.

## Copy allowance
0 to 6 words max, usually a single headline or no copy at all. The frame should carry the meaning first.

## Rejection triggers
- The product is not clearly visible in the first read.
- The scene depends on a fake environment or invented context.
- The frame needs a paragraph of text to explain what is happening.
- Motion is stronger than the subject and starts to feel promotional instead of premium.
