# Workflow Sequence

## Purpose
Show a step-by-step product flow, task progression, or before-to-after change in a controlled sequence.

## When to use
Use when the film must prove how the product works across multiple states or actions, not just show a single frame.

## Motion profile
Segmented beats, deliberate cuts, state transitions, and directional continuity. Keep the sequence calm and readable.

## Copy allowance
0 to 8 words total across the sequence. Use one short line per step only if the steps need reinforcement.

## Rejection triggers
- The sequence is only decorative and does not add functional understanding.
- Too many steps are packed into one shot and the result becomes noisy.
- The flow depends on imagined UI states or unsupported transitions.
- The timing is so fast that the viewer cannot track the progression.
