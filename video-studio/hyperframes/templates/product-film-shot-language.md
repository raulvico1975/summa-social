# Product Film Shot Language

## Purpose

This file defines the reusable shot grammar for premium Summa product films.

Use these jobs to build a proof-led sequence without falling back to a fixed
marketing template.

## Shot jobs

### `establish`

Open the workflow clearly and fast.
Show where the user is and why this moment matters.

### `orient`

Give the viewer the minimum context needed to read the next proof moment.
This is not a summary card.

### `isolate`

Direct attention to one control, field, row, warning, or result.
The camera move must make the proof easier to read, not more dramatic.

### `verify`

Show the state that proves the claim.
If the shot cannot prove anything, it is not a `verify` shot.

### `resolve`

Land the operational result.
The viewer should understand what changed and why that matters.

### `hold`

Stay just long enough for the viewer to absorb the result.
Do not use holds as filler.

## General rules

- one shot job per beat
- one proof target per beat
- one movement intention per beat
- no decorative camera choreography
- no typography-led beats when UI proof is available
