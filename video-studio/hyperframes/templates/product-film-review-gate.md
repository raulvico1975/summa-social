# Product Film Review Gate

## Purpose

Use this gate before treating any Summa product film as a serious public
candidate.

This is not a taste checklist. It is the minimum bar for approving or rejecting
a piece.

## Benchmark direction

The target is an editorial product film where:

- the real UI is the proof
- motion directs the eye instead of decorating the frame
- copy is sparse, calm, and operational
- the viewer understands the capability before the brand tries to impress them

Keep an OpenAI-like benchmark in mind:

- product-led, not campaign-led
- exact, not theatrical
- premium because the proof is well edited, not because overlays are dramatic

## Pass criteria

### 1. Product truth

- every beat is backed by a real approved capture
- the piece does not invent states, claims, or workflows
- the result frame proves the operational outcome

### 2. Readability

- the UI stays legible at website size
- the frame does not need pause to be understood
- text never competes with the proof area

### 3. Shot discipline

- each beat has one job and one proof target
- movement matches the shot job
- the piece can remove a beat without collapsing into confusion

### 4. Operational value

- a responsible economic user can explain why this helps real work
- the value is concrete: less risk, less manual review, more control, or faster closing
- the ending lands on usable outcome, not just aesthetic closure

### 5. Web quality

- the source capture is clean enough that reframing does not expose softness
- overlays support contrast without washing the product out
- the film still looks intentional when viewed frame by frame

## Automatic rejection triggers

- it feels like a poster laid over a screenshot
- the opening is broad context when a stronger proof state exists
- the piece relies on large typography to create importance
- the film is readable only because the UI has been obscured
- the story sounds generic enough to fit any SaaS product

## Review order

1. Capture quality
2. Beat structure
3. Copy
4. Motion
5. Export quality

If the capture fails, stop there and fix upstream.
