# Functional Explainer Contract

## Purpose

`functional-explainer` defines the proof-first master for Summa Social video.

Its job is to capture one real workflow clearly enough that the truth of the
story is locked before any premium editorial treatment begins.

It is upstream of `product-film` and upstream of all delivery cuts.

## Audience

The primary audience is a person who manages accounting, member administration, or operational follow-up inside a Catalan social entity.

The video must feel relevant to someone who:

- needs concrete workflow clarity
- does not want marketing abstraction
- cares about reducing manual ambiguity
- needs to trust what the product visibly changes

## Canonical shape

The canonical unit is a proof-first master:

`source capture -> functional-explainer master -> product film -> delivery cuts`

That means:

- the master is the source of truth
- the master does not need to look premium yet
- nothing downstream may invent a new story or new proof

## Mandatory traits

- real Summa UI captures are the primary proof
- one workflow only
- one proof target per beat
- low copy density
- explicit approval of what the piece proves and what it does not prove
- readable at normal playback

## Format target

- Initial target is `16:9`.
- Duration target is `18s` to `30s`.
- Beat target is `3` to `5`.
- One beat equals one proof job.

## Copy density

Copy density must stay low enough that the viewer can read the scene while watching at normal speed.

Rules:

- one dominant line per beat at most
- no decorative caption systems
- no lower-third template as default behavior
- if the UI proves the moment, copy should shrink

## Capture policy

Real product captures are mandatory whenever the piece is claiming product
behavior, workflow state, or outcome.

Illustration is optional support only.
It cannot replace proof.

## Rejection triggers

A functional explainer is rejected if any of the following are true:

- it feels like a generic SaaS ad
- it cannot be traced beat by beat to a real capture
- it asks the viewer to pause to understand the scene
- it tries to solve premium styling inside the proof layer
- it stacks too many text layers at once
- any scene loops, overlaps, restarts incorrectly, or obscures text

## First approved use

The first intended workflow for this channel is `importacio-extracte-conciliacio`.

That slice is a good first master because it is concrete, demonstrable, and naturally benefits from real UI capture.

## Relationship to product film

`functional-explainer` owns proof.
`product-film` owns premium public expression.

When they conflict, proof wins.

## Governance

This contract is part of the brand canon.
If a future explainer needs different rules, the rules must be written here before the studio runtime is treated as approved.
