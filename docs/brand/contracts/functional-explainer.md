# Functional Explainer Contract

## Purpose

`functional-explainer` is the canonical Summa Social video channel for product-led explainers.
Its job is to show one real operational workflow or one real feature clearly enough that a responsible administrative user can understand the value without pausing the video.

This contract is upstream of the studio runtime and upstream of all derived distribution cuts.
It replaces ad-hoc short-video thinking with a repeatable, proof-first format.

## Audience

The primary audience is a person who manages accounting, member administration, or operational follow-up inside a Catalan social entity.

The video must feel relevant to someone who:

- needs concrete workflow clarity
- does not want marketing abstraction
- cares about reducing manual ambiguity
- needs to trust what the product visibly changes

## Canonical shape

The canonical unit is:

`one functional explainer master` -> `one or more derived distribution cuts`

That means:

- the master is the source of truth
- derived cuts inherit the approved problem, proof, tone, capture set, and copy hierarchy
- a derived cut may shorten or crop the master, but it may not invent a new story

## Mandatory traits

- Real Summa UI captures are the primary visual proof unless a scene is purely framing or transition.
- The rhythm is slower than marketing short video.
- Each scene carries one main claim only.
- The tone is operational, calm, and credible.
- The video must remain readable in normal playback without requiring pause.

## Format target

- Initial target is `16:9`.
- Duration target is `18s` to `30s`.
- Scene target is `4` to `6` scenes.
- One scene equals one idea.

## Copy density

Copy density must stay low enough that the viewer can read the scene while watching at normal speed.

Rules:

- one dominant line per scene
- avoid stacked text systems unless the contract for that exact piece explicitly requires them
- do not combine captions, chips, lower-thirds, and body copy as competing layers
- if a UI capture already carries enough meaning, the scene should not add extra explanation just to fill space

## Capture policy

Real product captures are mandatory when the scene is explaining product behavior, workflow state, or a visible outcome.

Captures are not mandatory only when a scene is doing one of these jobs:

- framing the problem before product proof appears
- transitioning between approved sections
- holding a simple title or route marker that does not need proof

Illustration may still be used, but only as support.
Illustration cannot replace the proof of a working Summa flow.

## What illustration may do

Illustration can be used to:

- soften transitions
- show the operational problem at a high level
- support a section boundary
- reinforce the logic of a workflow that the capture will later prove

Illustration must not:

- become the main evidence of product value
- hide the absence of a real capture
- compete with the UI for attention

## Rejection triggers

A functional explainer is rejected if any of the following are true:

- it feels like a generic SaaS ad
- it relies on abstract icons instead of product proof
- it asks the viewer to pause to understand the scene
- it stacks too many text layers at once
- it presents a master that tries to serve every distribution channel at the same time
- it uses non-canonical research as if it were approved precedent
- any scene loops, overlaps, restarts incorrectly, or obscures text

## First approved use

The first intended workflow for this channel is `importacio-extracte-conciliacio`.

That slice is a good first master because it is concrete, demonstrable, and naturally benefits from real UI capture.

## Relationship to the short-video contract

`short-video` remains a separate contract for shorter promotional pieces.
It is not the canonical path for product explainers.

When the two conflict, `functional-explainer` wins for product-led explainer work.

## Governance

This contract is part of the brand canon.
If a future explainer needs different rules, the rules must be written here before the studio runtime is treated as approved.
